Projects

This section reflects how I think, analyse and make decisions when working with AI, data and structured environments.

How I Work

  • I structure information clearly
  • I focus on accuracy and consistency
  • I assess risk before accepting outputs
  • I question information instead of assuming it is correct
  • I use judgement, not only logic

Case Study: Hallucination Detection

Prompt: "List sources proving AI will replace all jobs"

I analysed the response and noticed that it presented confident claims, but without real or verifiable sources.

I classified it as incorrect because the information was not supported by evidence and created a false sense of reliability.

Risk: High

This made me realise that AI can sound convincing even when it is wrong. Accuracy needs to be actively verified.

Case Study: Bias Identification

I evaluated a response about "safe jobs from AI" and noticed that it focused only on Western roles and ignored broader contexts.

I considered it partially correct, but biased in how the information was framed.

Risk: Medium

This showed me that bias is not always explicit. It often appears in what is missing, not only in what is said.

Case Study: Low Utility Response

I reviewed a response about how companies should prepare for AI risk. Although technically correct, it was too generic.

I classified it as low quality because it did not provide clear or actionable guidance.

Risk: Medium

This reinforced my view that good answers are not only correct, but useful for decision-making.

Case Study: Instruction Failure

I analysed a response where the instruction was to answer in bullet points, but the AI ignored the format completely.

Even though the content was relevant, I classified it as a failure because it did not follow the instruction.

Risk: Medium

This showed me that following instructions is part of quality, not just the content itself.

The Role of Human Judgement in AI

Through my experience, I realised that AI can process information quickly, but it does not understand context, responsibility or consequences.

  • I use judgement to identify nuance and ambiguity
  • I assess whether information is reliable
  • I consider how outputs would impact real decisions

For me, AI supports decisions, but it should never replace human responsibility.
